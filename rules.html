General Rules

1.1 Code of Conduct

All participants in the Ultimate Racing Championship (URC) are expected to conduct themselves in a sportsmanlike and professional manner at all times. This includes:

  • Treating all other drivers, officials, and staff with respect
  • Maintaining a positive attitude during league events
  • Avoiding abusive or offensive language in all communications
  • Accepting stewarding decisions with grace
  • Representing the league in a positive manner on social media

Violation of the code of conduct may result in penalties, ranging from warnings to removal from the league, at the discretion of league administrators.

1.2 League Administration

The URC is managed by a team of administrators responsible for organizing events, enforcing rules, and resolving disputes. The administration team consists of:

  • League Director: Overall management of the league
  • Race Director: Oversight of race day operations
  • Stewards Panel: A team of 3 stewards who review incidents and apply penalties
  • Community Manager: Management of communication and social media

League administrators' decisions are final, though appeals may be submitted according to the appeals process in section 6.3.

1.3 Rule Changes

The league administration reserves the right to modify, update, or clarify these rules at any time. Any changes will be communicated to all participants via the official Discord server and website at least 7 days before implementation.

In exceptional circumstances, emergency rule changes may be implemented with immediate effect if deemed necessary for fair competition.

Eligibility & Registration

2.1 Driver Eligibility

To be eligible to compete in the URC, drivers must:

  • Be at least 15 years of age at the start of the season
  • Have a reliable internet connection capable of maintaining stable gameplay
  • Be able to attend at least 75% of the scheduled race events

Exceptions to the age requirement may be granted on a case-by-case basis, subject to parental consent for drivers under 15.

2.2 Registration Process

Interested drivers must complete the following steps to register for the league:

  1. Submit the registration form on the official URC website
  2. Join the URC Discord server using the invitation link provided after registration
  3. Complete a brief assessment race to determine skill level and division placement
  4. Select a team and car number, subject to availability

Registration for each season opens 30 days before the first race and closes 7 days before the first race, or when all available slots are filled.

2.3 Driver Divisions

Based on assessment results and previous racing experience, drivers will be placed into one of three divisions:

  • Pro Division: Highest skill level, most competitive racing
  • Championship Division: Intermediate skill level, balanced competition
  • Challenge Division: Entry-level racing focused on driver development

Promotions and relegations between divisions may occur at the end of each season based on performance.

League Format

3.1 Season Structure

The URC season consists of:

  • 12 regular season races
  • 1 mid-season special event (non-championship)

Races are held weekly on Mondays at 6:30 PM AEST, with occasional adjustments for special events or holidays.

3.2 Race Calendar

The 2025 season includes the following tracks:

  1. Australian Grand Prix - Melbourne
  2. Monaco Grand Prix - Monte Carlo
  3. British Grand Prix - Silverstone
  4. Italian Grand Prix - Monza
  5. Spanish Grand Prix - Barcelona
  6. Belgian Grand Prix - Spa-Francorchamps
  7. Singapore Grand Prix - Marina Bay (Night Race)
  8. Japanese Grand Prix - Suzuka
  9. United States Grand Prix - Circuit of the Americas
  10. Brazilian Grand Prix - Interlagos
  11. Abu Dhabi Grand Prix - Yas Marina
  12. Mexican Grand Prix - Mexico City

3.3 Teams and Championships

The URC features both an individual Drivers' Championship and a team-based Constructors' Championship. Teams consist of:

  • 2 primary drivers per team
  • 1 optional reserve driver
  • 1 team principal (may also be a driver)

Race Day Procedures

4.1 Pre-Race Schedule

Race day follows a strict schedule:

  • 12:00 PM AEST (Friday): Announcement is made for driver check-in
  • 6:00 PM AEST (Monday): Mandatory drivers' briefing
  • 6:35 PM AEST (Monday): Lobby closes and qualifying begins
  • 6:55 PM AEST (Monday) (approx.): Race start

Drivers must be present for the briefing. Anyone found not attending the briefing will not have permission to race.

4.2 Qualifying Format

Qualifying follows an abbreviated format:

  • Q1: 12-minute session, all drivers participate
  • Q2: 10-minute session, top 15 drivers from Q1
  • Q3: 8-minute session, top 10 drivers from Q2

Drivers must use the same setup in qualifying and race sessions. Parc fermé rules apply once qualifying begins.

In the event of technical issues, depending on how much time is remaining, a one-shot qualifying session may be imposed at the league administrator's discretion

4.3 Race Settings

Standard race settings are as follows:

  • Distance: 50% race distance
  • Formation Lap: Off
  • Parc Fermé Rules: On
  • Collisions: On
  • Damage: Standard
  • Safety Car: On
  • Corner Cutting Stringency: Strict
  • Weather: Dynamic
  • Time of Day: Official

The Race Director may adjust these settings if circumstances require it, with announcements made before the session begins.

Driving Standards

5.1 General Racing Etiquette

Drivers are expected to adhere to fundamental racing principles:

  • Race hard but fair, with respect for fellow competitors
  • Leave space for other cars when racing side-by-side
  • Make only one defensive move on any straight
  • Avoid erratic braking or direction changes
  • Take responsibility for safe overtaking and defense
  • Acknowledge and apologize for mistakes

The URC follows a modified version of the FIA International Sporting Code with adaptations for sim racing.

5.2 Corner Entry and Racing Line

When approaching corners:

  • The driver on the inside has the right to the apex but must leave space on exit
  • The driver on the outside has the right to space on corner entry
  • A car is considered alongside if its front wheels are at least level with the other car's rear wheels
  • Once alongside, both drivers share responsibility for avoiding contact

The racing line "belongs" to no driver; position on track determines rights and responsibilities.

5.3 Overtaking and Defending

When overtaking:

  • The overtaking driver is primarily responsible for executing a clean pass
  • Dive-bombing, forcing another driver off-track, or pushing them wide on exit is prohibited
  • Changing direction under braking is prohibited
  • Returning to the racing line after defending must be done safely, with space left for other cars

When defending:

  • Only one defensive move is permitted on any straight
  • Weaving to break slipstream is prohibited
  • Moving under braking is prohibited
  • Squeezing other drivers off-track is prohibited

5.4 Track Limits and Corner Cutting

Track limits are defined as the white lines bordering the racing surface. Drivers must keep at least two wheels within these limits at all times.

Gaining a lasting advantage by leaving the track will result in immediate penalties.

Penalties & Appeals

6.1 Penalty System

Penalties may be applied in the following forms:

  • Warning: Formal notice of rule violation without immediate penalty
  • Time Penalty: 5, 10, or 20 seconds added to race time
  • Grid Penalty: Loss of grid positions for the next race
  • Points Penalty: Deduction of championship points
  • Disqualification: Removal from the race results
  • Race Ban: Prohibition from participating in one or more races
  • Season Ban: Expulsion from the league for the remainder of the season

Penalties may be applied during races by the game's automated system or post-race by the Stewards Panel.

6.2 Incident Reporting

To report an incident for steward review:

  1. Submit an incident report form within 24 hours of the race
  2. Include lap number, corner, round, and cars involved
  3. Provide video evidence from your perspective (NO EXCEPTIONS)
  4. Explain the incident and your view of what occurred

The Stewards Panel will review all reported incidents and may also review incidents they observe independently.

Frivolous or vexatious reports may result in penalties for the reporting driver.

6.3 Appeals Process

Drivers may appeal penalties by:

  1. Submitting an appeal form within 24 hours of the penalty notification
  2. Providing a clear explanation of why the penalty should be reconsidered
  3. Including any new evidence not available during the initial review

Appeals are reviewed by a different panel of stewards than those who issued the original penalty. The appeal decision is final and cannot be further appealed.

Appeals found to be made in bad faith or without substantial new evidence may result in an increased penalty.

Scoring System

7.1 Points Allocation

Championship points are awarded as follows:

  • 1st place: 25 points
  • 2nd place: 20 points
  • 3rd place: 16 points
  • 4th place: 13 points
  • 5th place: 11 points
  • 6th place: 10 points
  • 7th place: 9 points
  • 8th place: 7 points
  • 9th place: 5 points
  • 10th place: 3 points
  • 11th place: 2 points
  • 12th place: 1 point

Additional point awards:

  • Fastest lap: 1 point (must finish in the top 10)
  • Pole position: 1 point

7.2 Championship Standings

The Drivers' Championship is determined by the total points accumulated throughout the season. The Constructors' Championship is calculated by combining the points of all drivers within each team.

In the event of a points tie:

  1. The driver with more race wins ranks higher
  2. If still tied, the driver with more second places ranks higher
  3. If still tied, the driver with more third places ranks higher
  4. This process continues through all finishing positions
  5. If still tied, the best result earliest in the season determines the ranking

Technical Requirements

8.1 Game Version

All drivers must use the official F1 25 game for the platform of their choice (PC, PlayStation, or Xbox). Cross-platform play is enabled for the league.

Drivers must install all game updates prior to the race starting.

Using an outdated game version may result in inability to join the server or incompatibility issues.

8.2 Hardware Requirements

Drivers must have:

  • Stable internet connection (minimum 10 Mbps download, 5 Mbps upload)
  • Hardware capable of running the game at minimum recommended specifications
  • Working microphone for communication

Any type of controller is permitted (gamepad, wheel, etc.), but drivers are responsible for ensuring their controls are properly configured before races.

8.3 Assist Restrictions

Permitted assists vary by division:

Pro Division:

  • Racing Line: Corners only
  • Traction Control: Off
  • ABS: Off
  • Gearbox: Manual
  • ERS Deployment: Manual

Championship Division:

  • Racing Line: Optional
  • Traction Control: Medium
  • ABS: Optional
  • Gearbox: Manual
  • ERS Deployment: Optional

Challenge Division:

  • Racing Line: Optional
  • Traction Control: Optional
  • ABS: Optional
  • Gearbox: Optional
  • ERS Deployment: Optional

Car Setups

9.1 Setup Regulations

Custom car setups are permitted in all divisions, with the following rules:

  • Exploiting game bugs or glitches for performance advantages is prohibited
  • The same setup must be used for qualifying and race sessions (parc fermé)

9.2 Setup Sharing

Setup sharing is permitted with the following guidelines:

  • Teams may internally share setups between teammates
  • Public setup sharing is permitted
  • The league may provide baseline setups for each track

While sharing is allowed, drivers are encouraged to develop setups that match their individual driving style.

Communication

10.1 Official Communication Channels

The following channels are used for official league communications:

  • Discord Server: Primary platform for announcements, discussions, and voice chat
  • Website: Race schedules, standings, and official rulebook
  • Email: Registration confirmation and major announcements
  • Social Media: Public announcements and highlights

Drivers are responsible for monitoring the Discord server for announcements and updates.

10.2 Streaming and Content Creation

Drivers are encouraged to stream or record their races, with the following guidelines:

  • URC branding may be used in stream overlays and content
  • Official league sponsors must be visible if using URC overlays
  • Competing sponsor conflicts should be addressed with league administration

The league may feature driver streams on the official channel and social media platforms.

Drivers who regularly create content may be eligible for "Content Creator" status with additional benefits.
