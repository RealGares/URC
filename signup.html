How to Join

Joining the Ultimate Racing Championship is a straightforward process. Complete the registration form, and our team will review your application. Once approved, you'll be placed in the appropriate division based on your experience level.

Requirements

To ensure a smooth racing experience for everyone, we have the following basic requirements:

  • A stable internet connection (minimum 10 Mbps download)
  • Discord account for communication
  • Ability to attend race sessions on Mondays at 6:30 PM AEST
  • Commitment to fair play and sportsmanship
  • Must be at least 15 years old

Important Information

Your application will be reviewed within 48 hours. If accepted, you'll receive an invitation to our Discord server with further instructions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the division placement work?
New drivers complete a brief assessment consisting of a hot lap and a short race simulation. Based on your performance and experience, you'll be placed in one of three divisions: Pro, Championship, or Challenge. This ensures competitive and balanced racing within each skill level.
What if I can't attend every race?
While regular attendance is encouraged, we understand that life happens. Drivers must attend at least 75% of races to remain in good standing. If you know you'll miss a race, simply notify your team principal or the race stewards in advance.
Are there any hardware requirements?
You need hardware capable of running the F1 25 game at stable framerates. While a wheel setup is recommended for optimal control, many successful drivers use gamepads.
How are teams formed?
New drivers can either join existing teams with vacancies or form new teams with other drivers. Each team consists of 2 primary drivers and can have a reserve driver.
Is there a registration fee?
No! Registration is completely free for everyone.
What happens if I break the rules?
We have a detailed rulebook and penalty system. First-time minor infractions typically result in warnings or small time penalties. Repeated violations may lead to grid penalties for future races. Serious misconduct such as intentional wrecking or abusive behavior can result in race bans or removal from the league.
